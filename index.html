One of the strange gifts of recovering from insomnia has been the sudden vividness of my dreams- whole worlds surfacing after a long silence. It makes me wonder how often the same occurs in waking life? How many perceptions and feelings pass unnoticed, not because they aren’t there, but because we move too quickly to notice? What kind of magic might reveal itself if we finally slowed down enough to let it arrive?

- Emily Anne Rose

I wrote this after reflection on the complexities of choice. I could not shake Sylvia Plath’s fig tree metaphor from The Bell Jar, where she confronts a multiplicity of paths and is paralyzed by the fear of losing all but one. I often find myself beneath my own fig tree, caught between the desire to have it all and the impossibility of doing so. This prose inhabits the space just before choice, serving as a reminder that even in the paralysis of possibility, there is a kind of beauty in the desire that brought us here.

- Emily Anne Rose
